You have found the Trappist-1 star system! This year, scientists discovered that 7 exoplanets are orbiting the Trappist-1 star, some 40 light years away from Earth. But that’s not what’s remarkable. More importantly, 5 out of these 7 exoplanets are located within the “habitable zone” of their star system, which means that they can potentially harbor life. If life were to exist there, it would be very different from life on Earth. First, the Trappist-1 star is red and casts a reddish glow onto the world. Another difference can be noticed when you stand on one of the planets and look up at the sky. You’ll see what appear to be 6 moons, but you’ll soon realize that they aren’t moons, but the 6 other neighboring planets. They appear to be as big as our moon because all the planets are much closer together than the planets in our Solar System.
