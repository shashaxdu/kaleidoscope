Leaving Earth

If you were to keep travelling at 25,000 mph, this would be an extremely long and dull journey. So, you’re going to start travelling at the speed of light. To understand how fast light travels, let’s look to the closest star to us, the Sun. Light from the Sun (and all light in the universe) travels at about 186,000 miles per second. 93 million miles away, light from the Sun takes about 8 minutes to reach the Earth. The Sun, then, is about 8 lightminutes away from Earth. (Don’t worry, you won’t be travelling to the Sun, since you would never survive that trip.)

Your first destination in space is the Moon, about 240,000 miles away from Earth. In 1969, Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin were the first humans to land on the Moon on the Apollo 11 Mission. Flying as fast as 25,000 mph on their spacecraft, they reached the Moon in about 75 hours.

You are now leaving Earth, the only home we’ve ever known. How big is the Earth, you wonder? It is 7,917 miles across in diameter and 26,000 miles in circumference. It would take you 47 hours to fly around the Earth in a typical airplane, which travels about 550 mph.
