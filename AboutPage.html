About This Project

Kaleidoscope is brought to you by Manar-ul Islam Swaby and Shasha X. Du, both current students in the Harvard Graduate School in Education. With a focus in Technology, Innovation and Education, both Manar and Shasha were inspired to design a product that would inspire young kids who are largely underrepresented within the fields of STEM. Kaleidoscope was developed for young girls and students of color as an entry point into contemplating and reflecting themes about their place in their social worlds as well as the larger universe.

Manar-ul Islam Swaby, Web Developer & UX Designer

Shasha X. Du, Visual, UX, & Learning Experience Designer

Manar-ul Islam is an educator, researcher and designer, who holds great value in exploring the ways in which information and knowledge systems are accessed, privileged and constructed. Manar's expertise in education and research fuel her design practice in creating experiences of understanding, ultimately bringing individuals into conversation with one another and their larger digital and physical worlds. At the heart of Manar's work is a strive towards equity. She aims to facilitate learning through a means of accessibility, agency and inclusion. Through all of her work, Manar aims to design experiences where learners can engage to construct meaning and ponder in reflection through a curious, yet critical lens.

Shasha is a designer of digital and real world experiences that encourage learning both lifelong and life-wide. Drawing inspiration from her interdisciplinary background and her experience studying, working, and traveling abroad, Shasha's design work explores the intersection between the arts, humanities, and sciences. Shasha has a BA in International Development Studies and is currently exploring UX, product, and learning experience design.
